Helsinki City Bikes

iOS App Redesign

Helsinki City Bikes started in 2016 in the heart of Helsinki. Personally I am a big fan of city bikes. Since the beginning, I have been using an app called “Helsinki Bikes”. Although it is not an official app, it has been very useful and often saves lots of my time. The app was developed by Anteneh Seifu. Because I have been using this app since 2016, I see many possibilities to make it better in terms of usability. At the end of this case study, I will send the new designs to the product owner/developer and suggest him if he wants to adapt to the new design.

Tasks

Research, User Flows, Information Architecture, Sketching, Wireframing, UI, Prototyping, User testing

Duration

2 weeks

Key Challenges

This app is not the official City Bike's app so there are limitations to it, such as payment, getting users' data and bike locking status.

High-level Goals

  • Redesigning the app with the user-centric approach.
  • Discover the user's pain point and solve it.
  • Make the app easy to use with minimum learning effort required.

Design Process

1. Background Research

I wanted to learn about how people are using city bikes, whether they depend on apps like Helsinki Bikes?How do they feel about it? What things they like about city bikes and how would they expect it to have it better? I conducted interviews with 11 regular users of city bikes who also use Helsinki Bikes app. The users were aged between 19 and 32. The questions focuses on their usage of city bikes and Helsinki Bikes app.

Following are the highlights:

User Pain Points

Being an active user myself and hearing all this feedback from other users, I found the following major pain points.

Difficult to browse

7 users preferred to have recent station locations stored in the app so they don’t have to worry about remembering the addresses and it is easy to navigate with one hand while riding the bike.

Time

Heading to any destination on a city bike hoping to find a station nearby is stressful and a huge problem. Often users have to look around and go a bit farther to return the bike.

Money

Heading to any destination on a city bike hoping to find a station nearby is stressful and a huge problem. Often users have to look around and go a bit farther to return the bike.

Penalty

3 of the users got a penalty because their bike was not locked.

Safety risks

9 of the users said it is difficult to ride when they have to check navigation from another app which also consumes their battery because of multiple apps running on the background.

Mounting phone

4 of the users said city bikes should have a mobile phone mount. This way they could focus on traffic while riding.

2. Opportunity

After understanding user's pain point and their expectations from the app, I saw following opportunities that can enhance the usability of the app and solve main problems users are facing.

3. Idea

My proposed Idea takes two of the main users' problems. While other opportunities look promising but the main problem most of the users are facing will be the priority. Also, from development point of view these are easier features to implement.

  • Add input search fields for a start point and end point, suggesting the route and nearest stations on both points.
  • Add notification for bike lock status especially if bike return is not successful.

    • Beside two main features, we can also have some more features which go along with above features.

  • Automatically save the recent input in local storage, and also give the option to add the latest searched location for future use.
  • Show users options to filter preferred routes.
  • Show users their trip history. (This feature is already available in HSL city bikes official account)

5. Validate

I conducted user testing on my mid-fidelity prototype to see how users would interact. As a tool I used clickable prototype from the Sketch App. Users' were delighted to see new features while having familiar UI. I was curious if they will understand and get along with updated interface. Main goal was to identify their pain points, how many task they are able to complete and overall satisfaction. Following were main tasks I tested:

  • Find bike station around
  • Search destination "Ravintola Cactus
  • Find best route
  • Navigate
  • Play around

6. Final Design

7.Measuring success

Currently, the app is free so I cannot measure how profitable it is. Instead, I would gauge how successful the design is through the following weekly/monthly activities:

  • App installed
  • Account created
  • The average time a user stays in the app
  • Use of notification
  • Average recent placed saved perusers

The proposed design solution was presented to users as a prototype. During the testing, most of the users said they would use the app more frequently if the app had these changes. Users were able to navigate better during the prototype test. It means the new design is successful.
Additionally, app can be monetized by partnering with different tourist attractions, bars, cafe, tour guide and more.
