Doctual

Responsive Web Application

Doctual was a legal tech platform that aimed to uberize legal documents. In short, Doctual was a platform between lawyers and startups. One could create a legal document such as employment contract, shareholders agreement, etc. in a few minutes by answering questions whereas lawyers could create a template and get income every time their template was used. The software was sold and Doctual went for liquidation and is no more in operation. I will highlight here only a part of the journey as I am bound to NDA.

My role

Research, User Flows, Information Architecture, Sketching, Wireframing, UI, Prototyping, User testing, Creating product roadmap

Duration

February 2017 to November 2018

Challenges

In a small startup, getting user’s feedback in every step of the process was not easy and the feedback was limited. To cover that part, I read a similar product’s users review and tried to understand the pain points and discussed those with users available to be a part of Doctual’s user research.

High-level Goals

  • Design a platform that allows users to easily create legal document online, send for attorney review, invite parties to review and sign
  • Discover and implement the important features
  • Make application easy to use with minimum learning effort required

Design Process

Discovery

To achieve the design goal, we started the project by defining the underlying causes of the lack of proper legal documentation in startups by desk research and user study. Our research included market reviews, benchmarking, interviews and field research in different startup hubs, startup events in Finland as well as on different social media groups.

After collecting and analyzing the data, we conducted a co-creation workshop with stakeholders to further define the product requirement and understand the way it should work.

Field research was great for gathering information of targeted audience, while the interviews allowed me to gain a deeper understanding of the participant's behaviors. The main takeaways from the two methods were:

Startup is a vague term. We had to segment startups in order to find out true users of Doctual. We realized that startups who are about 6–24 months old are our ideal customers.

Those are the startups who have a limited budget, who understand the importance of legal documents but cannot afford the high fees of lawyers and they are most likely to use Doctual. Once a startup gets to Venture Capital(VC) round or gets investment from VC, they afford to hire a lawyer and they tend to feel more secure with a real lawyer who is present physically.

Meet Andrei

I used the findings to construct a provisional persona, Andrei, to help me understand how I can help him/our users achieve their goal using Doctual. Meet Andrei, a 32-year old entrepreneur who has a startup that recently got government funding and is expecting to grow in the Nordic market.

Prototype concept

We opted for a lean approach that emphasized rapid sketching, prototyping, user feedback, and design mockups. This created early team‐wide alignment, sparked tons of great ideas and created a strong sense of ownership. This gave everyone a clear idea of what we are building next.

Doctual Version 1

The first version of Doctual was intended for startups. The workflow was easy to follow. It was based on user’s feedback. Following points were the main features of the first version:

  • Select the document template
  • Answer all the questions attached to the template
  • Preview the document and make the necessary correction
  • Invite lawyers for review
  • Invite parties to review and sign
  • Sign the document and store it for future usages.
